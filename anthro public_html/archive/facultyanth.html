ANTHROPOLOGY FACULTY

 

Deborah Blom, dblom@zoo.uvm.edu Assistant Professor, came to UVM in 1998. She received her B.S. from the University of Houston and her M.A. and Ph.D. from the University of Chicago. She has done field work in Mesoamerica, Andean South America, and the U.S. She specializes in archaeology and biological anthropology and is interested in the study of human skeletal remains in archaeological contexts, New World complex societies, health and nutrition, ethnicity, and the ways in which humans modify their bodies as a means of expressing identity. To learn more about Professor Blom and the courses she teaches, visit her website at http://www.uvm.edu/~dblom/.

John Crock, jcrock@zoo.uvm.edu Assistant Research Professor, Director of the Consulting Archaeology Program, came to UVM in 2000. He received his B.A. from the University of Vermont, and his Ph.D. from the University of Pittsburgh. He has conducted archaeological research in Maine, Vermont and the British West Indies. His research interests include maritime adaptations, the development of chiefdoms and prehistoric networks of trade and exchange. To learn more about the Consulting Archaeology Program, visit their website at http://www.uvm.edu/~uvmcap/.

Jennifer Dickinson, jadickin@zoo.uvm.edu Assistant Professor, came to UVM in 2002. She received her A.B. degree in Anthropology and Russian from Bryn Mawr College in 1992, and has two M.A. degrees, in Anthropology and Russian and East European Studies, from the University of Michigan. She received her Ph.D. in Anthropology from the University of Michigan in 1999. She has done field work in many areas of the former Soviet Union, and specializes in Ukraine, particularly the Zakarpattja border region. Her academic interests include linguistic anthropology, visual anthropology, and the anthropology of work. Her most recent research has combined analysis of conversational speech, socio-economic transition, and advertising and media. To learn more about Professor Dickinson and the courses she teaches, visit her website at http://www.uvm.edu/~jadickin/.

Robert Gordon, rgordon@zoo.uvm.edu Professor, came to UVM in 1979. He received his B.A. and M.A. from the University of Stellenbosch and his Ph.D. from the University of Illinois. His field work has been primarily in Southern Africa and New Guinea. In Namibia he studied mineworkers and Bushman genocide. His theoretical interests include politics and law, visual and urban anthropology, development in third world and culture change.

James Petersen, jpeterse@zoo.uvm.edu Associate Professor and Chair, came to UVM in 1997. He received his B.A. from the University of Vermont and his Ph.D. from the University of Pittsburgh. He has done extensive archaeological field work in northern New England, Montana, various Caribbean islands and the Brazilian Amazon. His interests range from ecological anthropology to material culture and the native peoples of the western hemisphere.

Jeanne Shea, jlshea@zoo.uvm.edu Assistant Professor, came to UVM in 1998. She received her B.A. in Asian Studies from Dartmouth College and her M.A. and Ph.D. in Social Anthropology from Harvard University. Her research interests focus on China, the U.S.A., health, gender, ethnicity, class, the lifecourse, generations, economic development and social change. To learn more about Professor Shea and the courses she teaches, visit her website at http://www.uvm.edu/~jlshea/.

Luis Vivanco, lvivanco@zoo.uvm.edu Assistant Professor, began teaching at UVM in 1999. He received his B.A. in Religion from Dartmouth College and his M.A. and Ph.D. from Princeton University in Cultural Anthropology. His research is on the socio-cultural dynamics of nature conservation in rural Costa Rica. He is also interested in social studies of knowledge, science and technology; the anthropological study of international development; U.S. race, ethnicity and environmental justice; and the anthropological study of tourism. He acts as the department's Internship Coordinator. To learn more about Professor Vivanco and the courses he teaches, visit his website at http://www.uvm.edu/~lvivanco/.
