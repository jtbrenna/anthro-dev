MINOR REQUIREMENTS IN ANTHROPOLOGY

 

Students who are not majoring in anthropology have the option of selecting among three (3) potential concentrations for a minor in anthropology:

  • Social anthropology
  • Archaeology
  • Sociolinguistics

A concentration in social anthropology requires a student to take Human Cultures (Anth 21), two topical courses and one "peoples" course or two "peoples" courses and one topical course. They must take one course at the 200-level, excluding advanced courses in archaeology (Anth 200, 210) and readings and research (Anth 297, 298).

A concentration in archaeology requires a student to take Prehistoric Archaeology (Anth 24) and Archaeological Theory (Anth 210) and three courses from the following group: North American Indians (Anth 160), Cultures of South America (Anth 161), Historical Archaeology (Anth 188), Field Work in Archaeology (Anth 200), and Museum Anthropology (Anth 250).

A concentration in sociolinguistics requires a student to take Linguistic Anthropology (Anth 28), Sociolinguistics (Anth 178), and two of the following: North American Indians (Anth 160), Cultures of South America (Anth 161), Cultures of Africa (Anth 162), South Pacific Cultures (Anth 163), Peoples of South Asia (Anth 165) or Peoples of the Middle East (Anth 166); and one of the following: Microethnography (Anth 284) or Cross-Cultural Communication (PSYC 237).
