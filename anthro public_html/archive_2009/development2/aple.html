ACADEMIC PROGRAMS FOR LEARNING AND ENGAGEMENT (APLE)

Anthropology students at UVM are provided opportunities to work with different faculty and others beyond UVM to gain "hands-on" experience in the field. These opportunities are briefly explored below under two categories, Research and Internships.

RESEARCH:

The research conducted by UVM anthropology faculty is rather diverse and various students have been involved with faculty research in the recent past. Recent anthropology research involving students has included the study of prehistoric archaeological collections from the Caribbean islands of Anguilla and Montserrat, along with archaeological collections from Maine and Vermont, for example. Other recent anthropology students have been involved in osteological analysis of human remains from both Bolivia and Vermont. These and other research opportunities are ongoing for anthropology students. Generally, research opportunities are available to advanced students in the different subdisciplines of anthropology. Students should contact individual faculty members with whom they are interested in doing research and/or contact the department chair.

INTERNSHIPS:

Internship opportunities for UVM anthropology students are potentially even more diverse than faculty research collaboration opportunities, including internships through UVM and beyond UVM as well. Potential internships at UVM include work with the UVM Consulting Archaeology Program doing a gamut of field and/or laboratory activities, for example. Recent anthropology internships beyond UVM have included work with the Vermont State Archaeologist, Vermont Division for Historic Preservation, in Montpelier, for example. There are many other potential opportunities for internships, depending on the student's background and interest in anthropology, although these too are generally done by advanced students. Interested students should contact Luis Vivanco, who serves as the Internship Coordinator for the Department of Anthropology.
