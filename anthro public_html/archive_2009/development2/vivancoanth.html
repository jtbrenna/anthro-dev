Luis Vivanco, lvivanco@zoo.uvm.edu Assistant Professor, began teaching at UVM in 1999. He received his B.A. in Religion from Dartmouth College and his M.A. and Ph.D. from Princeton University in Cultural Anthropology. His research is on the socio-cultural dynamics of nature conservation in rural Costa Rica. He is also interested in social studies of knowledge, science and technology; the anthropological study of international development; United States race, ethnicity and environmental justice; and the anthropological study of tourism. He acts as the department's Internship Coordinator. To learn more about Professor Vivanco and the courses he teaches, visit his web site at http://www.uvm.edu/~lvivanco/.
