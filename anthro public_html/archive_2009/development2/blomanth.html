Deborah Blom, dblom@zoo.uvm.edu Assistant Professor, came to UVM in 1998. She received her B.S. from the University of Houston and her M.A. and Ph.D. from the University of Chicago. She has done field work in Mesoamerica, Andean South America, and the United States. She specializes in archaeology and biological anthropology and is interested in the study of human skeletal remains in archaeological contexts, New World complex societies, health and nutrition, ethnicity, and the ways in which humans modify their bodies as a means of expressing identity. To learn more about Professor Blom and the courses she teaches, visit her web site at http://www.uvm.edu/~dblom/.
