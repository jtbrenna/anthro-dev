Jennifer Dickinson, jadickin@zoo.uvm.edu Assistant Professor, came to UVM in 2002. She received her A.B. degree in Anthropology and Russian from Bryn Mawr College in 1992, and has two M.A. degrees, in Anthropology and Russian and East European Studies, from the University of Michigan. She received her Ph.D. in Anthropology from the University of Michigan in 1999. She has done field work in many areas of the former Soviet Union, and specializes in Ukraine, particularly the Zakarpattja border region. Her academic interests include linguistic anthropology, visual anthropology, and the anthropology of work. Her most recent research has combined analysis of conversational speech, socio-economic transition, and advertising and media. To learn more about Professor Dickinson and the courses she teaches, visit her web site at http://www.uvm.edu/~jadickin/.
