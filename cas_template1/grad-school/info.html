Where Can I Get More Information?

Office of Fellowship Advising
You can find out about grants - for post-undergrad research or for graduate studies - from this office.

American Anthropological Association
The definitive source for links to websites treating all the subdisciplines of anthropology, including socio-cultural, archaeological, biophysical, linguistic and applied. They have constantly updated links to different websites of anthropological interest. You should especially check out their career pages, where they list available jobs, as well as the essential American Anthropological Association Guide to Departments, an annually published tome that lists faculty and basic information on all departments. We have one in the department office. Extremely helpful for finding out who is in what departments and what they work on. You can also find the American Anthropological Association Newsletter to get a sense of the state of the discipline, the job market, funding opportunities, etc. Most faculty receive this and it is on-line through the AAA website.

Society for American Archaeology
For information particular to archaeology.

American Association of Physical Anthropology
For information particular to biological anthropology.

The Real Guide to Grad School: What to Know Before You Choose
Produced by the editors of Lingua Franca, the academic society pages.

Individual Department Websites
Where you'll get the concrete info you need on specific programs.
